Drug List

    Alprazolam

    Class: Sedative-Hypnotic

    Sub-class: Benzodiazepine

    MOA: acts by facilitating the binding of the inhibitory neurotransmitter GABA
    at various GABA receptors throughout the CNS

    Uses:

    1. In Anxiety Neuroses
      • Anxiety with depression
      • Anxiety with panic disorder
    2. In Insomnia
    3. Treatment of alcohol withdrawal

    Adverse effects:

    1. Hangover
    2. Hypersensitivity
    3. Tolerance & Dependence
    4. Poisoning (in higher doses)

    Atropine

    Class: Anticholinergic/ Antimuscarinic drug

    Sub-class: Natural Alkaloids

    MOA: It inhibits the muscarinic actions of acetylcholine on structures innervated by postganglionic cholinergic nerves and on smooth muscles

    Uses:

    1. To reduce saliva & fluid in the respiratory tract during surgery
    2. To treat bradycardia
    3. To increase respiratory rate
    4. Used in CPR

    Adverse effects:

    1. Bigger, dilated or enlarged pupils
    2. Chest pain, discomfort or tightness
    3. Decrease in urine volume
    4. Dryness of mouth
    5. Blurred vision, photophobia
    6. Tachycardia

    Amoxicillin

    Class: Antibiotic drug

    Sub-class: Penicillin like antibiotic

    MOA: Exerts bactericidal activity via inhibition of bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding one or more of the penicillin binding proteins(PBPs)

    Uses:

    1. To treat bacterial infections
    2. Chest infections like pneumonia
    3. Dental abscesses
    4. H. pylori induced ulcers
    5. Ear infections in children

    Adverse effects:

    1. Skin rash
    2. Itching
    3. Shortness of breath
    4. Swelling of hands, face, mouth or throat
    5. Nausea, vomiting, Diarrhoea

    Azithromycin

    Class: Antibiotic drug

    Sub-class: Macrolide antibiotic

    MOA: The macrolides and ketolides bind irreversibly to a site on the 50S subunit of thr bacterial ribosome, this inhibiting translocation steps of protein synthesis, inhibition of proinflammatory cytokine production, inhibition of neutrophil infestation & macrophage polarization alteration

    Uses:

    1. Bronchitis
    2. Pneumonia
    3. Sexually transmitted infections
    4. Infections of the ears, lungs, sinuses, skin, throat & genitals

    Adverse effects:

    1. Nausea, vomiting
    2. Diarrhoea
    3. Loss in appetite
    4. Dizziness and fatigue
    5. CVS disturbances
    6. Tachycardia, Angina
    7. Blistering and body ache

    Aspirin

    Class: Non steroidal antiinflammatory drugs(NSAIDs)

    Sub-class: Acetylsalicylic acid drug

    MOA:Inhibit the activity of cyclooxygenasr(COX) which leads to the formation of prostaglandins (PGs) that cause inflammation, swelling, pain and fever

    Uses:

    1. As analgesic
    2. As antipyretic
    3. As anti inflammatory
    4. As blood thinner
    5. In heart attacks & strokes

    Adverse effects:

    1. Abdominal & stomach pain, burns & cramps
    2. Reye's syndrome
    3. Chest pain & discomfort
    4. Constipation
    5. Difficult breathing
    6. Dizziness
    7. Kidney problems

    Atenolol

    Class: Beta blocker drug

    Sub-class: Selective beta blocker

    MOA: It is a competitive antagonist that blocks the beta-1 adrenergic receptors in the heart, causing heart to beat slower and less forcefully

    Uses:

    1. High blood pressure
    2. Angina
    3. Heart attack
    4. Irregular heart rhythm
    5. Prevention of migraine

    Adverse effects:

    1. Bradycardia
    2. Heart failure
    3. Peripheral vascular insufficiency
    4. Raynaud's syndrome
    5. Worsening of AV block
    6. Worsening of heart failure

    Amlodipine

    Class: Calcium channel blockers or Calcium antagonists

    Sub-class: Dihydropyridine drugs

    MOA: blocks the voltage dependent L- type calcium channels, thereby inhibiting the initial flux of calcium into vascular smooth muscle cells and myocardial cells.

    Uses:

    1. Hypertension
    2. To prevent heart diseases
    3. To prevent strokes
    4. To prevent angina

    Adverse effects:

    1. Headache
    2. Upset stomach
    3. Nausea
    4. dizziness
    5. Swelling of hands, feet, ankles or lower legs

    Atorvastatin

    Class: Hypolipidemic drugs

    Sub-class: HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors(Statins)

    MOA: competitively inhibits 3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-coenzyme A (HMG-CoA) reductase, by preventing the conversion of HMG-CoA to mevalonate, statin medications decrease cholesterol production in the liver.

    Uses:

    1. Lower cholesterol and triglyceride(fats) levels in the blood
    2. To prevent fat clogging in blood vessels
    3. Heart diseases

    Adverse effects:

    1. Arthralgia
    2. Dyspepsia
    3. Nausea
    4. Diarrhoea
    5. Nasopharyngitis
    6. Insomnia
    7. Urinary tract infection
    8. Pain in extremities
