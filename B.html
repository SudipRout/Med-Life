Drug List

    Budesonide

    Class: Corticosteroids

    Sub-class: Inhalation corticosteroid

    MOA: It is a long acting beta2 agonist bronchodilator that decreases resistance in the respiratory airway and increases airflow to the lung

    Uses:

    1. Bronchial asthma

    Adverse effects:

    1. Abdominal pain and indigestion
    2. Headaches
    3. Myalgias
    4. Skin rashes and acnes
    5. Weight gain
    6. Discomfort or white spots in the mouth or food pipe

    Bupivicaine

    Class: Local Anaesthetic

    Sub-class: Amide group of LA

    MOA: It blocks the Na+ channels and the generation and conduction of nerve impulses by increasing the threshold for electrical excitation in the nerve, by slowing the propagation of the nerve impulse and by reducing the rate of rise of the action potential.

    Uses:

    1. As local anaesthetic in caudal, epidural and spinal anaesthesia
    2. To manage acute and chronic pain

    Adverse effects:

    1. Cardiotoxicity
    2. Systemic effects like tachycardia and hypertension
    3. Conduction block and asystole

    Buprenorphine

    Class: Opioid

    Sub-class: Amide group of LA

    MOA: It acts as a partial mixed opioid agonist at the mu receptor and as an antagonist at the kappa receptor.

    Uses:

    1. Analgesic in acute and chronic pain
    2. Opioid dependance

    Adverse effects:

    1. Nausea
    2. Vomiting
    3. Drowsiness
    4. Dizziness
    5. Headache
    6. Memory loss
    7. Sweating
    8. Dry mouth
    9. Miosis
    10. Orthostatic hypotension
    11. Sexual side effects
    12. Urinary retention
