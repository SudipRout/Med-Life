Drug List

    Dexamethasone

    Class: Corticosteroids

    Sub-class:

    MOA: It acts by suppressing the migration of neutrophils and decreasing lymphocyte colony proliferation

    Uses:

    1. Inflammation
    2. Allergies
    3. Asthma
    4. Arthritis
    5. Sclerosis

    Adverse effects:

    1. Abdominal and stomach cramps
    2. Bloody, black, tarry stools
    3. Cough or hoarseness
    4. Skin rashes and acnes
    5. Diarrhoea
    6. Weight gain
    7. Rashes and acne

    Diclofenac

    Class: Non steroidal anti inflammatory drugs

    Sub-class: Phenylacetic acid

    MOA: It acts by the inhibition of prostaglandin synthesis by inhibiting cycloxygenase-1(COX-1) and cyclooxygenase-2(COX-2) with relative equipotency.

    Uses:

    1. To treat mild to moderate pain
    2. Osteoarthritis
    3. Rheumatoid arthritis
    4. Inflammation
    5. Stiffiness

    Adverse effects:

    1. Stomachache
    2. Nausea and Vomiting
    3. Diarrhoea
    4. Headache
    5. Drowsiness
    6. Peptic ulcer
    7. Tinnitus

    Dicyclomine

    Class: Anticholinergic drug

    Sub-class:

    MOA: It inhibits the muscarinic actions of acetylcholine on autonomic nerve endings, decreasing gastrointestinal secretions and intestinal motility

    Uses:

    1. To treat symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome
    2. Muscle spasms
    3. Biliary colic
    4. Stomach and intestinal cramping

    Adverse effects:

    1. Dry mouth
    2. Vomiting
    3. Upset stomach
    4. Dizziness
    5. Constipation
    6. Abdominal pain
    7. Bloating
    8. Loss of appetite
    9. Dizziness
