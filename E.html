Drug List

    Ethosuccimide

    Class: Antiepilectic drug

    Sub-class:

    MOA: It works by the antagonism of the postsynaptic T type voltage gated calcium channel

    Uses:

    1. Petit mal seizures
    2. Anticonvulsant
    3. Childhood absence epilepsy

    Adverse effects:

    1. Nausea
    2. Vomiting
    3. Diarrhoea
    4. Anorexia
    5. Drowsiness
    6. Lethargy
    7. Insomnia

    Ephedrine

    Class: Adrenergic drug

    Sub-class: Phenylacetic acid

    MOA: It is a direct and indirect sympathomimetic amine. As a direct effect, ephedrine activates alpha adrenergic and beta adrenergic receptors. As an indirect effect, it inhibits norepinephrine reuptake and increases the release of notephinephrine from vesicles in nerve cells

    Uses:

    1. Hypotension
    2. Shortness of breath
    3. Chest tightness
    4. Wheezing due to bronchial asthma

    Adverse effects:

    1. Tachycardia
    2. Nausea
    3. Anxiety
    4. Insomnia
    5. Restlessness
    6. Tremor
    7. Dry mouth
    8. Impaired circulation to extremities
    9. Headache
    10. Cardiac arrythmias
    11. Tolerance

    Erythromycin

    Class: Macrolide antibiotic

    Sub-class:

    MOA: It prevents the growth of bacteria by inhibiting their protein synthesis

    Uses:

    1. Respiratory tract infections
    2. Skin infections
    3. Diphtheria
    4. Intestinal amebiasis
    5. Acute pelvic inflammatory diseases
    6. Legionnaire's disease
    7. Pertussis
    8. Syphilis

    Adverse effects:

    1. Nausea
    2. Vomiting
    3. Diarrhoea
    4. Abdominal cramps
    5. Loss of appetite
    6. Bloating and indigestion
