Drug List

    Haloperidol

    Class: Conventional antipsychotics

    Sub-class:

    MOA: It competitively blocks post-synaptic dopamine (D2) receptors in the brain, eliminating dopamine neurotransmission and leading to the relief of delusions and hallucinations

    Uses:

    1. It is used to treat nervous, emotional, and mental conditions (eg, schizophrenia)
    2. It is also used to control the symptoms of Tourette's disorder

    Adverse effects:

    1. Dizziness
    2. Lightheadedness
    3. Fainting

    Hydralazine

    Class: Vasodilators

    Sub-class:

    MOA: It interferes with calcium transport to relax arteriolar smooth muscle and lower blood pressure

    Uses:

    1. High blood pressure (Hypertension)
    2. Heart attack
    3. Stroke

    Adverse effects:

    1. Headache
    2. Pounding/fast heartbeat
    3. Loss of appetite
    4. Nausea and vomiting
    5. Diarrhea
    6. Dizziness

    Hydrocortisone

    Class: Corticosteroid

    Sub-class:

    MOA: It inhibits accumulation of inflammatory cells at inflammation sites, phagocytosis, lysosomal enzyme release and synthesis, and release of mediators of inflammation

    Uses:

    1. Redness
    2. Swelling
    3. Itching
    4. Discomfort of various skin conditions

    Adverse effects:

    1. Dizziness
    2. Headache
    3. Swollen ankles
    4. Tiredness
