Drug List

    Carbamazepine

    Class: Anticonvulsants

    Sub-class:

    MOA: Stabilizes hyperexcited nerve membranes, inhibits repetitive neuronal discharges and reduces synaptic propagation of excitatory impulses.

    Uses:

    1. Epilepsy
    2. Trigeminal neuralgia
    3. Maniac and Bipolar disorder
    4. Alcohol withdrawal syndrome
    5. Restless leg syndrome

    Adverse effects:

    1. Sedation and dizziness
    2. Vomiting and diarrhoea
    3. Hypersensitivity reactions
    4. Visual disturbance
    5. Teratogenic

    Carbidopa

    Class: Decarboxylase inhibitor

    Sub-class: Amide group of LA

    MOA: Focused on peripheral DDC as this drug cannot cross the blood-brain barrier. Hence, it will prevent the metabolism of levodopa in the periphery but it will not have any activity on the generation of dopamine in the brain.

    Uses:

    1. Parkinson's disease
    2. Postencephalitic parkinsonism
    3. Symptomatic parkinsonism followed by carbon monoxide or manganese intoxication

    Adverse effects:

    1. Headache and dizziness
    2. Jerky or twisting muscles
    3. Low BP
    4. Sleep problems
    5. Dry mouth
    6. Nausea, Vomiting, Constipation

    Celecoxib

    Class: NSAIDS

    Sub-class: COX-2 inhibitors

    MOA: Selective inhibition of cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), which is responsible for prostaglandin synthesis, an integral part of the pain and inflammation pathway. This pharmacologic activity gives celecoxib its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic

    Uses:

    1. Acute pain or menstrual pain
    2. Ankylosing spondylitis or osteoarthritis
    3. Rheumatoid arthritis or juvenile rheumatoid arthritis
    4. Acute migraine with or without aura

    Adverse effects:

    1. Bleeding gums
    2. Peripheral edema
    3. Heartburn
    4. Dizziness
    5. Diarrhoea
    6. Hypertension

    Cholecalciferol (Vit.D3)

    Class: Vitamin D analogs

    Sub-class: COX-2 inhibitors

    MOA: Occurs in two steps , the first hydroxylation of vitamin D3 cholecalciferol (or D2) occurs in the liver to yield 25-hydroxyvitamin D while the second hydroxylation happens in the kidneys to give 1, 25-dihydroxyvitamin D

    Uses:

    1. Rickets
    2. Osteoporosis
    3. Osteomalacia
    4. Vitamin D deficiency
    5. Hypoparathyroidism

    Adverse effects:

    1. Skin rash
    2. Vomiting
    3. Anorexia
    4. Headache and confusion
    5. Kidney and heart damage
    6. Bone weakness

    Cholnidine

    Class: Antihypertensives

    Sub-class: Centrally acting alpha agonists

    MOA: Acts by stimulating the pre-synaptic alpha 2 adrenoceptors, thereby decreasing noradrenaline release from both central and peripheral sympathetic nerve terminals.

    Uses:

    1. High BP
    2. Severe dysmenorrhea
    3. Vascular migraine headaches
    4. Alcohol withdrawal symptoms
    5. To control diarrhoea in diabetic patients with nephropathy

    Adverse effects:

    1. Dry eyes and mouth
    2. Dizziness and headache
    3. Insomnia
    4. Constipation
    5. Sedation
    6. High BP

    Chlorpromazine

    Class: Antipsychotics (phenothiazine)

    Sub-class: Aliphatic side chain

    MOA: The antiemetic effect of chlorpromazine stems from the combined blockade of histamine H1, dopamine D2, and muscarinic M1 receptors in the vomiting center. Chlorpromazine is extensively metabolized by the liver.

    Uses:

    1. Schizophrenia
    2. Psychotic disorder
    3. Maniac phase of bipolar disorder
    4. Behavioural problem in children

    Adverse effects:

    1. Blurred vision
    2. Dizziness and lightheadedness
    3. Dry mouth
    4. Nausea, Constipation, Weight gain
    5. Fast heartbeat

    Cimetidine

    Class: First generation antihistaminic drug

    Sub-class: H2 blocker

    MOA: Competitively blocks histamine from stimulating the H2-receptors located on the gastric parietal cells (these cells are responsible for hydrochloric acid secretion and secretion of the intrinsic factor)

    Uses:

    1. Peptic and stress ulcers
    2. Heartburn
    3. Reflux disease
    4. Zollinger Ellison syndrome

    Adverse effects:

    1. Headache
    2. Insomnia
    3. Muscle pain
    4. Slurred speech
    5. Gynaecomastia
